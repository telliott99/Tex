\[ \int \frac{\ln x}{x^2} \ dx \]
Let
\[ u = \ln x \]
\[ du = \frac{1}{x} \ dx \]
\[ dv = \frac{1}{x^2} \ dx \]
\[ v = -\frac{1}{x} \]
We get 
\[ \int \frac{\ln x}{x^2} \ dx = -\frac{\ln x}{x} + \int \frac{1}{x^2} \ dx \]
\[ = -\frac{\ln x}{x} - \frac{1}{x} \]
\[ = -\frac{1}{x} \ (\ln x + 1) \]
Evaluate from $x=1 \Rightarrow x=2$:
\[ =  -\frac{1}{2} \ (\ln 2 + 1) +  1 \]
